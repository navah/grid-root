welcome to navah.site :)

hi, my name is navah (NA-vah, rhymes with 'lava'). this website is a showcase (or storage space) for projects and activities.

i am particularly interested in numerical modeling and visualizing information.

my PhD research (University of Chicago, 2020) applied tried-and-true numerical methods to new questions at the intersection of climate sciences and seafloor geology. my other scientific exploits include mantle dynamics and evolutionary biology.

if you came here looking for JokesByDad, Ted Cruz fanfiction, Ben Bernenke fanfiction, or Climate Shawty, i'm sorry to report that those projects have been retired. however, i am still the climate shawty and am happy to help with any climate science questions.

data viz.

spider plots

I used d3 (Data-Driven Documents) to make an interactive web-based visualization of various tropes found in movies on the Lifetime network.


Lifetime is television for women. MMISWP (a podcast on the Solid Listen network) uses a proprietary algorith to evaluate Lifetime movies. Well, more like an evaluation based on a list of classic tropes. They don't go from 'good' to 'bad', they go from 'real movie' to 'Lifetime-y'.


But how do individual Lifetime movies compare to each other? I used d3 (Data-Driven Documents) to make an interactive web-based visualization for podcast listeners to get some perspective. Here are some screenshots!

science viz.

geotopics

Illustrated Topics in Geophysics is a printed zine I made to teach people about climate science! I distributed it around Chicago!


These are non-consecutive pages from a zine about carbon and the climate. Now that I have a Wacom drawing tablet, I need to re-ink the artwork when I have some downtime, then I'll make the whole thing a PDF.


After reading all 12 pages, I hope the reader generally understands what carbon is doing in the atmosphere, and what it means to be a greenhouse gas. Nothing too technical.

graphics

Graphics I've made have ended up on the web, on print materials, on t-shirts, and on many many many stickers.


Rossbypalooza brought climate scientists and statisticians together for a few intensive summer weeks to collaborate! The visuals I made for merch and badges feature atmospheric fluid dynamicist Carl-Gustof Rossby (1898-1957). His pipe was iconic.



Menus and misc. other printed materials for La Penultima in San Juan, Puerto Rico feature a tetrahedral kite. Alexander Graham bell loved tetrahedral kites, and so do the owners of the bar.
